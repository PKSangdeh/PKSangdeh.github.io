A Spectrum Sharing Scheme for CRNs: Design and Experiments
IEEE/ACM Transactions on Networking

We proposed a spectrum sharing scheme for cognitive radio networks where secondary users have no knowledge about the primary network. We have built a prototype of our scheme that showed the secondary network could coexist with commercial Wi-Fi and LTE devices without degrading their performance.

Joint work with Hossein Pirayesh, Adnan Quadri, and Huacheng Zeng.

A Practical Downlink NOMA Scheme for WLANs
IEEE Transactions on Communications


We proposed a downlink NOMA scheme for WLANs including precoder design, user grouping, and successive interference cancellation (SIC). We prototyped the proposed scheme and experimental results show that the proposed downlink NOMA signiﬁcantly improves the weak user’s date rate and sum rate.

Joint work with Hossein Pirayesh and Huacheng Zeng.

A Practical Underlay Spectrum Sharing Scheme for CRNs
IEEE Internet of Things Journal


WiFi-IoT coexistence adds energy-efficient IoT communications into WLANs. WiFi-IoT features two innovative techniques: an asymmetric physical (PHY) design and a transparent coexistence scheme. TCCI: Taming Co-Channel Interference for Wireless LANs
MobiHoc 2020

TCCI is an interference management scheme enabling concurrent transmission in WLANs. TCCI requires neither network-wide synchronization nor inter-network data sharing, and therefore is amenable to implementation. We have illustrated TCCI compatibility with commercial devices.

Joint work with Adnan Quadri, Hossein Pirayesh, and Huacheng Zeng.

A Practical Underlay Spectrum Sharing Scheme for CRNs
INFOCOM 2019


This paper proposes a practical underlay spectrum sharing scheme for CRNS where the primary users are oblivious to secondary users. The key components of our scheme are two MIMO-based interference cancellation techniques, blind beamforming and blind interference cancellation techniques.

Joint work with Hossein Pirayesh, Hongxiang Li, and Huacheng Zeng.

SDoF of two-user X-channel with synergistic alternating CSIT
IET Information Security

In this paper, a two-user SISO X-channel with confidential messages is addressed. During different time slots, the CSIT alternates between three states including perfect, delayed, and null. Several schemes capable of attaining the maximum achievable SDoF are presented.

Applying Byzantine Agreement in WSNs based on clustering
ICEE 2015

Due to large communication overhead, Byzantine Agreement (BA) degrades the lifetime of WSNs dramatically. For detecting defective or malicious sensors, the traditional BA does not offer a feasible solution. In this paper, we propose a two-level leader-first BA for large scale WSNs to reduce the energy consumption and maintain the reliability of network.

Joint work with Forough Poursabzi and Mahtab Mirmohseni.

IET Information Security

In this paper, a two-user SISO X-channel with confidential messages is addressed. During different time slots, the CSIT alternates between three states including perfect, delayed, and null. Overview of multiplexing techniques in wireless networks
IntechOpen

In this chapter, we review the multiplexing techniques that are used in exiting wireless systems, including TDMA, CDMA, FDMA, OFDM, and power-domain NOMA. The ultimate goal of these techniques are providing the opportunity of concurrent multi-user transmissions and effective utilization of available communication resources.

Joint work with Huacheng Zeng.

Secure Degrees of Freedom of two-user two-hop X-channel
ICEE 2017

In this paper, SDoF of two-user two-hop X-channel is investigated. We consider blind transmitters alongside a relay having access to alternating CSI, and there exists feedback from one of the receivers to the relay. We present the optimal SDoF and achievable schemes for this case.

Joint work with Bardiaya Barari and Bahareh Akhbari.

Blind interference alignment for three-user multi-hop SISO interference channel
ICUMT 2014

In this paper, we investigate the three-user multi-hop SISO interference channel. The relays do not have CSI and they use AF relaying strategy. With the aid of reconfigurable antennas at receivers, a blind scheme is presented to achieve the optimal DoF without CSIT.

Joint work with Mahtab Mirmohseni.

Interference alignment for two-user two-hop interference X-channel with delayed and No CSIT
ICUMT 2014

In this paper, we investigate the DoF in the two-user two-hop Interference X-channel (2×2×2-X) under two regimes of CSI availability: delayed CSIT and no CSIT. We present IA scenarios for each of these regimes which achieve the optimal Dof of 4/3.

Joint work with Mahtab Mirmohseni.
